Scott Frost

Nebraska Quarterback 1995-1997

Scott Frost hurdles over a defender en route to the 1997 national championship over Peyton Manning's Tennessee

Here's a timeline of Scott's life, time as a player, and coaching career:

  • Born Jan 4, 1975 in Wood River, Nebraska
  • Wins two varsity letters at Stanford University in 1993/1994 before transferring to Nebraska in 1995
  • Immortalizes himself in Husker lore with the "Flea Kicker" play in 1996 to defeat Missouri
  • Wins the 1997 national championship by trouncing Tennessee 42-17
  • Selected in the 3rd round of the 1998 NFL draft. Retires from the NFL in 2003
  • Serves as a graduate assistant for Nebraska from 2002-2006
  • Takes position as member of Northern Iowa staff from 2007-2008
  • Joins the Oregon coaching staff in 2009, is promoted to offensive coordinator in 2013 where he serves until 2015. Helps break up the infamous Oregon-Boise St fight on national television
  • Accepts position as the head coach of UCF
  • TBD: returns to coach his alma mater Nebraska as they return to glory

"I'm bringing the whole playbook"

-Scott Frost, 2016
